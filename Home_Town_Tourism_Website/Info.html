About Polson

This scenic town by a lake that serves as a hub for a fertile farming area in Montana.

Polson attracts many tourists due to its location on the southern edge of Flathead Lake, the largest freshwater lake in the western US. Popular outdoor activities including boating, fishing, and camping also bring many visitors. The local economy heavily depends on tourism, with hotels, restaurants, and stores all thriving because of it.

Polson has a thriving agricultural industry featuring farms and ranches, with a focus on producing highly desired fruits like apples and cherries.

Residents can find employment opportunities in food processing, wood products, and plastics.
